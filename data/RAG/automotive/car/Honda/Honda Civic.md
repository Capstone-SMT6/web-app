# Honda Civic

The Honda Civic is a line of cars manufactured by Honda. Originally a subcompact, the Civic has gone through several generational changes, becoming both larger and more upscale, moving into the compact car segment.

## Variants

### 1. Standard Civic (The Commuter)
* **Body Style:** Available in sedan, hatchback, and coupe bodystyles over the years.
* **Performance:** Typically front-wheel drive (FWD) with fuel-efficient 4-cylinder engines, sometimes naturally aspirated and sometimes turbocharged.
* **Reputation:** Renowned for its reliability, excellent fuel economy, and practicality.

### 2. Civic Si (Sport Injected)
A sport compact version of the Civic.
* **Drive:** FWD, usually equipped with a limited-slip differential.
* **Engine:** Upgraded, higher-revving 4-cylinder engines paired exclusively with a manual transmission.
* **Legacy:** A staple in tuning culture, balancing performance and daily usability at an accessible price point.

### 3. Civic Type R (The Track Weapon)
The highest performance version of the Civic.
* **Drive:** Advanced FWD system with a sophisticated chassis tuning.
* **Engine:** Turbocharged 2.0L producing over 300 horsepower.
* **Legacy:** Holds numerous lap records for FWD cars worldwide, celebrated for retaining practical hatchback utility while rivaling sports cars on tracks.

## Status
Currently in its 11th generation, the Civic remains one of the best-selling automobiles globally.
