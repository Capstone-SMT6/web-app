# Mitsubishi Lancer

The Mitsubishi Lancer is a compact sedan produced by Mitsubishi Motors from 1973 to 2017. It is widely recognized for its duality: a reliable everyday commuter and a high-performance rally icon.

## Variants

### 1. Standard Lancer (The Commuter)
* **Body Style:** Primarily a 4-door sedan, though some generations included wagons and hatchbacks (Sportback).
* **Performance:** Typically front-wheel drive (FWD) with fuel-efficient 4-cylinder engines.
* **Reputation:** Known for being an affordable, dependable, and simple vehicle for daily driving.

### 2. Lancer Evolution (The Icon)
Often shortened to "Evo," this version transformed the Lancer into a world-class performance car.
* **Drive:** Features a sophisticated All-Wheel Drive (AWD) system.
* **Engine:** Powered by a turbocharged 2.0L engine (notably the 4G63 or 4B11T).
* **Legacy:** Gained legendary status in the World Rally Championship (WRC) and pop culture (e.g., Fast & Furious, Initial D).
* **Generations:** There are 10 official versions (Evo I to Evo X).

### 3. Lancer Ralliart (The Middle Ground)
A "warm" version that sits between the base model and the Evo. It offers more power and AWD (on later models) but with a more comfortable ride and lower price point than the full Evolution.

## Status
Mitsubishi discontinued the Lancer globally in 2017 to focus on SUVs and Crossovers, though the name persists in some specific markets like Taiwan.
