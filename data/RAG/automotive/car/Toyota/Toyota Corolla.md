# Toyota Corolla

The Toyota Corolla is a line of subcompact and compact cars manufactured by Toyota. Introduced in 1966, the Corolla became the best-selling car worldwide by 1974 and has been one of the best-selling cars in the world since then.

## Variants

### 1. Standard Corolla (The Commuter)
* **Body Style:** Available as a sedan, hatchback, and previously as a station wagon.
* **Performance:** Typically front-wheel drive (FWD) with extremely relatable, fuel-efficient 4-cylinder engines. Hybrid powertrains are also highly popular.
* **Reputation:** Universal symbol of reliability, ease of maintenance, and longevity. The quintessential default car for a massive demographic.

### 2. GR Corolla (The Hot Hatch)
Developed by Toyota's Gazoo Racing (GR) division, turning the everyday hatchback into an aggressive performance vehicle.
* **Drive:** GR-FOUR All-Wheel Drive (AWD) system with selectable power distribution.
* **Engine:** Turbocharged 1.6L 3-cylinder engine (G16E-GTS) producing around 300 horsepower.
* **Legacy:** A modern rally-inspired hot hatch highly praised for its engaging driving dynamics and manual-only transmission.

### 3. AE86 (The Drifting Legend)
A specific rear-wheel-drive generation from the 1980s (Corolla Levin and Sprinter Trueno).
* **Drive:** Rear-Wheel Drive (RWD), which made it a low-cost favorite in motorsport.
* **Legacy:** Immortalized in the anime *Initial D* and essentially birthed modern drift culture with the help of drivers like Keiichi Tsuchiya.

## Status
The Corolla continues to thrive, now in its 12th generation, offering a massive array of trims, hybrid options, and the newly added performance variant.
